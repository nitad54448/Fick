1 Thermodynamics: The $L$ vs. $D$ Matrix

A common point of confusion is the symmetry of the interaction matrix. This tool uses concentration gradients, leading to an asymmetric diffusion matrix.

The Onsager Matrix ($L$)

$$ J_i = - \sum_{j} L_{ij} \nabla \mu_j $$

Driven by Chemical Potential ($\mu$).
Symmetric ($L_{ij} = L_{ji}$) due to microscopic time reversibility.

The Diffusion Matrix ($D$)

$$ J_i = - \sum_{j} D_{ij} \nabla C_j $$

Driven by Concentration ($C$).
Asymmetric ($D_{ij} \neq D_{ji}$) because $D = L \cdot \frac{\partial \mu}{\partial C}$.

2 The Manning-Darken Approximation

This tool calculates $D_{ij}$ using the Manning-Darken formalism for vacancy-mediated diffusion. The asymmetry arises physically from the Vacancy Wind Effect.

Term Formula Physical Meaning
Diagonal ($D_{ii}$) $$D^*_i + C_i (D^*_{matrix} - D^*_i)$$ Tracer diffusion modified by the net flow of the matrix.
Off-Diagonal ($D_{ij}$) $$C_i (D^*_{matrix} - D^*_j)$$ Vacancy Wind: If species $j$ is fast, it creates a vacancy wind that drags species $i$. This is asymmetric because $C_i \neq C_j$.

3 Numerical Algorithm & Stability

We solve the coupled PDEs using an Explicit Finite Difference Method. Because the equations are coupled, simple stability checks fail.

New: Spectral Radius Control

To prevent numerical explosions, we dynamically calculate the timestep $\Delta t$ using Gershgorin's Circle Theorem to estimate the maximum Eigenvalue ($\lambda_{max}$) of the diffusion matrix:

$$ \Delta t \leq \frac{\Delta x^2}{2 \cdot \lambda_{max}} \quad \text{where} \quad \lambda_{max} \approx \max_i \sum_j |D_{ij}| $$

5 Non-Ideal Systems (Gamma $\neq$ 1)

This simulation assumes a Dilute Solution ($\gamma \approx 1$), meaning entropy is the only driver. In concentrated alloys, Enthalpic interactions (repulsion/attraction) modify the flux via the Thermodynamic Factor ($\Phi$):

$$ D_{actual} = D_{onsager} \cdot \underbrace{\left( 1 + \frac{\partial \ln \gamma}{\partial \ln C} \right)}_{\Phi} $$
Repulsion
$\Phi > 1$

Atoms hate each other.
Diffusion Accelerates.

Attraction
$0 < \Phi < 1$

Atoms order/clump.
Diffusion Slows Down.

Spinodal
$\Phi < 0$

Phase Separation.
Negative Diffusion (Unmixing).

* Note: Spinodal decomposition ($\Phi < 0$) causes standard Fickian solvers to crash or oscillate.
